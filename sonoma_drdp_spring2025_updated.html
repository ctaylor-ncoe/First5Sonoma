Percentage LEA/IEP, IFSP vs Community by Age

Key Findings - Age Distribution

Age 0 (61% LEA/IEP, IFSP): High LEA/IEP, IFSP representation reflects early intervention (Part C) capturing identified delays early, while community infant care is underrepresented.

Age 2 (89% Community): The gap between Part C ending and Part B beginning at age 3, with community toddler programs dominating.

Ages 3-5 (~50/50): Reflects expansion of both preschool options and Part B services after age 3.

Age 5 (58% LEA/IEP, IFSP): Children still receiving services who haven't yet transitioned to kindergarten.

Domain Readiness: 5-Year-Olds in Community Programs

Key Findings - Kindergarten Readiness

Physical Development (98%): Nearly all children demonstrate kindergarten-ready motor skills and physical health indicators.

Language & Cognition (89%, 83%): Strong readiness in receptive/expressive language and mathematical thinking.

Social-Emotional & Self-Regulation (72%, 69%): Areas with room for growth; these foundational skills support classroom success.

Literacy (57%): Lowest readiness domain; reflects the developmental nature of emergent literacy skills in preschoolers.

Data Source: Combined Sonoma County DRDP Assessment Data
